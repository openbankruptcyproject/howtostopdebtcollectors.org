Debt Collection by Text and Email

The 2021 CFPB rules allow collectors to contact you by text and email - but with important restrictions

The 2021 Regulation F Changes

In November 2021, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau issued Regulation F, updating debt collection rules for the digital age. For the first time, the rules explicitly address contact by text message, email, and social media. Collectors may now use these electronic channels, but with significant restrictions designed to protect consumer privacy.

The rules require collectors to include an opt-out mechanism in every electronic communication - a way for you to tell the collector to stop using that particular channel. Once you opt out of text messages, for example, the collector must stop texting you.

Email Collection Rules

Collectors can send emails to any email address you previously used to communicate about the debt, or to an email address the collector reasonably believes belongs to you. However, they must include a clear opt-out mechanism in every email. They also cannot send emails to work email addresses if they know the employer prohibits it.

A major concern with email collection is privacy - emails can be seen by others who have access to your account. The rules attempt to address this by limiting the information disclosed in the subject line and requiring that the debt details be contained in the body of the message or an attached document.

Text Message Restrictions

Collectors can text you at phone numbers you previously used to communicate about the debt. They must identify themselves as a debt collector in the message and include opt-out instructions. You can opt out of text messages while still allowing phone calls, or vice versa.

Each text message must be limited in what it reveals. The collector cannot include the full debt amount in a text message viewable on a lock screen, as this could reveal the debt to anyone who sees your phone.

Your Right to Opt Out

You can opt out of any electronic communication channel at any time. If you tell a collector to stop texting, they must stop texting. If you tell them to stop emailing, they must stop emailing. You can force all communication into writing (postal mail) by opting out of calls, texts, and emails.

Sending a comprehensive cease and desist letter that covers all channels is the most effective way to stop unwanted electronic contact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can debt collectors text me?

Yes, under the 2021 Regulation F rules, but they must include opt-out instructions in every text and cannot reveal debt details visible on a lock screen.

Can I opt out of texts but still get calls?

Yes. You can opt out of individual communication channels. Opting out of texts does not affect the collector ability to call or send mail.

Can collectors contact me on social media?

Under Regulation F, collectors can send private messages on social media, but cannot post publicly about your debt. They must identify themselves as debt collectors and include opt-out instructions.

What if a collector keeps texting after I opt out?

This is a violation of Regulation F and the FDCPA. Document the continued texts (screenshots with timestamps) and file complaints with the CFPB and your state attorney general.
